Manna Gum (*Eucalyptus viminalis*) is one of Melbourne's most recognisable gum trees, with its striking smooth white bark and characteristic habit of growing along waterways. Ribbons of shed bark often drape through the canopy.

The name "manna" comes from the sweet substance produced by sap-sucking insects on the leaves — a food source for sugar gliders and an important resource in Aboriginal food culture.

You'll recognise Manna Gum by its smooth, white upper trunk and branches, contrasting with a rough grey stocking of bark at the base. The leaves are narrow and the same colour on both sides, unlike many other eucalypts.

This is one of Melbourne's most important koala food trees. Where Manna Gums grow along creek corridors, they form vital wildlife corridors connecting larger bushland areas.

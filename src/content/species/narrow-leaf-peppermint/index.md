Narrow-leaf Peppermint (*Eucalyptus radiata*) is the source of one of the best field identification tricks: crush a leaf between your fingers and smell. The strong peppermint scent is unmistakable and unique among Melbourne's eucalypts.

This is a medium-sized tree of Melbourne's eastern hills, where it grows in wetter forests alongside Messmate Stringybark and Mountain Ash. The bark pattern is distinctive — rough and fibrous at the base, transitioning to smooth pale grey on the upper trunk and branches.

The leaves are fine and narrow, giving the canopy a delicate, feathery appearance quite different from the broader-leaved eucalypts. Buds appear in unusually large clusters of up to 30.

Narrow-leaf Peppermint essential oil has been harvested commercially in Australia since the nineteenth century. The species remains an important source of eucalyptus oil.

Red Box (*Eucalyptus polyanthemos*) is immediately recognisable by its distinctive round juvenile leaves — a feature that persists on young growth even on mature trees. The name *polyanthemos* means "many flowers."

You'll find Red Box on drier hills and ridgetops to Melbourne's west and north. It favours well-drained, often rocky soils and is a common tree in the grassy woodlands that once covered much of Melbourne's volcanic plains.

The bark is classic box type: short-fibred, grey, persistent on the trunk and larger branches. Unlike stringybark, box bark doesn't peel in strips — it has a more compact, interlocking texture.

Red Box is an important species in Melbourne's remaining grassy woodlands, an ecosystem now critically endangered due to urban expansion and agriculture.

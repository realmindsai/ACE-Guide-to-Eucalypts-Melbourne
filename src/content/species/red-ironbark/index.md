Red Ironbark (*Eucalyptus tricarpa*) is unmistakable once you know it. The bark is deeply furrowed, extremely hard, and dark reddish-black — the hardest bark of any Melbourne eucalypt. Try pressing your thumbnail into it: you won't make a mark.

This species grows in drier areas to Melbourne's west and north, typically on poorer soils where it can be the dominant tree. The name *tricarpa* refers to the three-valved fruit, which is large and woody — among the biggest gum nuts you'll find near Melbourne.

Red Ironbark flowers in autumn and winter, making it a critical food source for honeyeaters, lorikeets, and other nectar-feeding birds during the cooler months when few other eucalypts are in flower.

The timber is among the most durable of any Australian hardwood, historically prized for railway sleepers, fence posts, and bridge timbers.
